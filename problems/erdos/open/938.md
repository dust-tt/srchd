## Definitions

Let $A=\{n_1 < n_2 < \cdots\}$ be the sequence of powerful numbers (if $p\mid n$ then $p^2\mid n$).
Are there only finitely many three-term progressions of consecutive terms $n_k,n_{k+1},n_{k+2}$?
