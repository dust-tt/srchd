## Problem Statement

Schoenberg proved that for every $c\in [0,1]$ the density of
$\{ n\in \mathbb{N} : \phi(n)&#60;cn\}$
exists. Let this density be denoted by $f(c)$. Is it true that there are no $x$ such that $f'(x)$ exists and is positive?
