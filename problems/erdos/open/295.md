$N ≤ n_1 < ⋯ < n_k$ with $\frac 1 {n_1} + ... + \frac 1 {n_k} = 1$.

Let $k(N)$ denote the smallest $k$ such that there exists
$N ≤ n_1 < ⋯ < n_k$ with $\frac 1 {n_1} + ... + \frac 1 {n_k} = 1$

Is it true that $\lim_{N \to \infty} k(N) - (e - 1)N = \infty$?
