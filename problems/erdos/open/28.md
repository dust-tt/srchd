## Problem Statement

If $A\subseteq \mathbb{N}$ is such that $A+A$ contains all but finitely many integers then $\limsup 1_A\ast 1_A(n)=\infty$.

## Background

Conjectured by Erdős and Tur&#225;n. They also suggest the stronger conjecture that $\limsup 1_A\ast 1_A(n)/\log n&#62;0$. Another stronger conjecture would be that the hypothesis $\lvert A\cap [1,N]\rvert \gg N^{1/2}$ for all large $N$ suffices. Erdős and S&#225;rk&#246;zy conjectured the stronger version that if $A=\{a_1&#60;a_2&#60;\cdots\}$ and $B=\{b_1&#60;b_2&#60;\cdots\}$ with $a_n/b_n\to 1$ are such that $A+B=\mathbb{N}$ then $\limsup 1_A\ast 1_B(n)=\infty$.
