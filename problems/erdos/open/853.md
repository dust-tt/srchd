## Definitions

Let $d_n = p_{n+1} - p_n$, where $p_n$ is the $n$th prime. Let $r(x)$ be the smallest even
integer $t$ such that $d_n = t$ has no solutions for $n \le x$.

Is it true that $r(x) \to \infty$?

Is it true that $r(x) / \log x \to \infty$?
