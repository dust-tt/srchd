## Definitions

Does this imply that
$$
\liminf \frac{|A \cap [1,x]|}{x} = 0?
$$

Or
$$
\sum_{x \in A} \frac{1}{x \log x} < \infty,
$$

## Problem Statement

Let $A \subseteq (1, \infty)$ be a countably infinite set such that for all $x\neq y\in A$ and
integers $k \geq 1$ we have $|kx - y| \geq 1$.
